Jerry Baldwin - Zev Siegl - Gordon Bowker

The co-founders of Starbucks are Jerry Baldwin, Zev Siegl, and Gordon Bowker. They opened the first Starbucks store in Seattle's Pike Place Market in 1971. However, the person who is often credited with turning Starbucks into the global brand it is today is Howard Schultz, who joined the company in 1982 and served as CEO from 1987 to 2000 and again from 2008 to 2017. Schultz was instrumental in expanding Starbucks from a small Seattle-based coffee roaster and retailer into an international coffeehouse chain.
